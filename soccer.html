Soccer Robot

A team of maximum three participants will need to build three types of robots. The robots can function as a goalkeeper, a defender and a striker. This game is similar to the soccer game where participants will control the three robots manually to compete with another team. The game will last for 4 minutes and 30 seconds with two periods of 2 minutes each. Half time intervals is 30 seconds. Team with more number of goals win. As for the tiebreaker, penalty-kicks will be offered. Points to go for next round depends on the win point, lose and draw point.

Categories: Primary and Secondary School

Soccer Robot FAQ

Q1: Will the game fields be elevated or be placed on floor?

A1: On floor.

Q2: How far are the operators to the game field? (Concern of the wireless communication range between remote control and robot)

A2: Operators can stand right beside the game field, but not at the side of goal. The maximum distance from operator to robot should be 4 meters.

Q3: The "box" used for measurement of the robots are rectangles or round?

A3: The measuring box of robot dimension is square shape with the given maximum size; it will be a cube which include the height measurement.

Q4: To decide winner for penalty kick, can different robots used to kick the ball?

A4: Yes, can use different robot.

Q5: When a foul occurs, who can touch the ball and robot?

A5: The ball will be placed by the referee, but the player cannot have any physical contact with robot, robot can only be controlled remotely.

Q6: Do I need to remove the robot if the robot is damaged or be immobilized?

A6: The game will be paused and the robot is need to removed or replaced with another robot and placed at last position. Call time-out to replace the robot.

Q7: How long the goalkeeper can catch or hold the ball?

A7: Goalkeeper is allowed to catch the whole ball. A goalkeeper can only hold the ball not more than 10 seconds.

Q8: Can the player push the ball and the opponent at the same time?

A8: It is permitted to push the ball and the opponent provided the pushing player is always in contact with the ball and also face to face with the opponent, not from side or back of opponent.

Q9: Can a robot have 2 legs for kicking the ball? For example, it can kick the ball from rear and front.

A9: Yes. As long as the extended dimension does not exceed the maximum robot size.

Q10: For free kick, kicker need to pass the ball to teammate or it can carry the ball on its own?

A10: the ball can be passed, carried or kicked.

Q11: For penalty kick, who will place the ball?

A11: Referee will place the ball, but player can relocate the ball.

Q12: What if the ball cannot be seen clearly on white line?

A12: We will change the color of the ball to yellow.

Q13: What happen if the ball flies over the goal?

A13: If it passes higher than the wall. It will not be counted.

Q14: Does the prizes consist of our registration fee?

A14: No. all of our prepared prizes are from sponsor.
